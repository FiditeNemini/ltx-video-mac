## 1. Diner Scene — "Maybe It Is a Spirit"

**Genre:** Cinematic drama, dialogue-driven  
**Tone:** Warm exhaustion, the hush after something impossible

```
A quiet roadside diner at dusk, wood‑paneled walls, amber pendant light over a black‑coated booth, vinyl booths, slatted blinds casting horizontal amber bars across the room; a mild hum of refrigeration, subtle floor‑scratching, wind chimes. Two coffee cups sit on a wooden table; the old man (70 yr old, white hair, denim jacket, quiet, small smile, deep, American English) sits on the left, his hands resting on the table, elbows bent, eyes down, glancing at his coffee, while the son (early‑40s, flannel over t‑shirt, warm, curious, American English) leans forward across the table, elbows on the surface, staring at his father. Camera: medium two‑shot, 35 mm, slight dolly in from 0‑4 s, then cuts to medium close‑up 50–85 mm, back to two‑shot 35–50 mm.
Old man (quiet, calm, American English): "I've seen one before. A long time ago."  Son (low, measured, American English): "When?"  Old man (soft, nostalgic, American English): "The night your mother passed."  Son (tears in eyes, whisper, American English): "…"  Old man (warm, gentle, American English): "Maybe it is a spirit."
```

## 2. Desert Highway — "Watch for the Tall Beings"

**Genre:** Cinematic thriller / sci-fi undertone  
**Tone:** Uneasy calm, dry heat haze, something unsaid beneath routine

```
Scene tone: Uneasy calm, dry heat haze, something unsaid beneath routine. Cinematic realism, desaturated warm tones — bleached sky, tan dust, faded asphalt. Late afternoon, Nevada desert highway. No other cars. No wind.

START FRAME (0–2.5s): Medium close-up (50mm) from the driver's POV looking out the open driver-side window. A police officer leans down into frame — mirrored aviators, thick brown moustache, beige uniform pressed flat. His cruiser idles behind, light bar off. Heat shimmer ripples off the road beyond his shoulder. Officer (steady, matter-of-fact): "License looks fine. But I need you to keep your speed down through here." Motion: Officer shifts weight slightly, one hand resting on the car door frame.

JUMP CUT 1 (2.5–5s): Hard cut to a tight close-up (85mm) of the officer's face — just below the aviators to the moustache. His jaw tightens almost imperceptibly. A bead of sweat traces his temple. Officer (dropping half a register, quieter): "And I'm gonna tell you something else. Watch for the tall beings out in these parts." Motion: Micro push-in. His eyes flick once toward the open desert behind the car, then back.

JUMP CUT 2 (5–7.5s): Hard cut to reverse angle — close-up (50–85mm) of the driver's face through the window frame. Mid-30s, slightly sunburned, plain t-shirt. Expression shifts from mild confusion to a cautious nod. He swallows once. Driver (measured, respectful): "Ok sir, thank you." Motion: Single slow nod. Hands visible at the bottom of frame, gripping the steering wheel a little tighter.

JUMP CUT 3 (7.5–10s): Hard cut to a wide close-up (35–50mm) from behind the driver's car — the officer stepping back, one pat on the roof. He turns and walks toward his cruiser. The empty two-lane highway stretches into a vanishing point. Desert floor on both sides — flat, pale, infinite. In the far distance, heat distortion makes a vague vertical shape shimmer and dissolve. No dialogue. Only the faint idle of both engines and dry silence. Motion: Officer walks away slowly. Camera holds static. The shimmer in the distance lingers.
```
